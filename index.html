How It Works

Welcome to Expense Tracker 👋

Your personal finance companion — log every rupee, understand your habits, plan your goals. Everything stays on your device. No account. No ads. Just you and your money.

What Each Tab Does
➕
Add Entry
Your main screen for logging money. Three panels side by side — Expense, Income, Investment — each showing your 5 most recent entries. Quick Repeat chips at the top pre-fill your most-used expenses in one tap. Use ⊕ Split to divide one expense across multiple categories. Scroll down to manage Recurring transactions and Bill Reminders. Spending alerts appear at the top when you are close to a budget limit.
📅
Daily View
See your spending day by day for any month. Tap a bar in the chart to see all transactions for that day, or use the Previous / Next buttons to step through. Category Avg chips let you combine any categories and see their average daily cost. The calendar heatmap shows intensity at a glance. Habit Analysis at the bottom scores your consistency and patterns out of 100.
🗂
All Transactions
A searchable, sortable full-history table of every entry. Filter by type, category, or month. Sort any column by clicking its header. Tap ✎ to open the edit modal and change any field. Tap ✕ to delete — a confirmation dialog always appears so nothing is removed by accident. Export filtered results as a CSV.
📊
Dashboard
Analytics for any month or all time. The Money Flow card shows Income → Spent → Invested → Net Saved. Key metrics include avg daily spend and spend pace vs last month. A category donut, top-7 expense list, and a 12-month trend line are shown side by side. When a specific month is selected you also get a month-over-month comparison. A Financial Health score and all-time Lifetime Summary sit at the bottom.
🎯
Budget Planner
Set a spending limit per category for any month. Progress bars turn orange at 80% and red when over budget. Spending alerts on the Add Entry tab warn you in real time. Use the Print / Share button to export a formatted budget report as a PDF.
🤝
Money & Goals
Two sections under one tab, switchable via the sub-nav. IOwe — track money you lent or borrowed. Add the person, amount, and optional due date. Tap 💬 Nudge to record a follow-up. Mark Settled when done. Goals — savings buckets for things you are working toward. Set a target amount, a monthly plan, and a deadline. Deposit money as you save and watch the progress bar fill. A 🎉 banner fires when you hit the target.
⚙️
App Settings
Everything in one place. Data — export as JSON (full backup) or CSV (for Excel / Sheets). Import JSON to restore, or drag-and-drop a CSV to bulk-add transactions. Categories — add custom categories with any emoji. Delete unused ones (a dialog shows how many transactions use the category before removing). Appearance — toggle dark or light mode and choose from 6 colour themes: Green, Ocean, Rose, Amber, Mint, or Violet.
Getting Started — 5 Steps
1️⃣
Log your first income — Go to Add Entry, use the Income panel, and enter your salary or any money coming in. This sets your net balance baseline.
2️⃣
Add expenses as you go — Every time you spend, log it immediately. Tap the + button in the bottom-right corner for the fastest route — takes under 10 seconds.
3️⃣
Set a budget — After a week of logging, go to Budget Planner and set limits for your top categories. You will see alerts on the Add Entry tab as you approach them.
4️⃣
Review on Dashboard — Pick the current month and study the Money Flow card and category donut. See what is eating your budget and compare it to last month.
5️⃣
Back up regularly — Go to App Settings and export a JSON backup. Save it to Google Drive or email it to yourself. Import on any device to restore everything instantly.
Power Tips
⚡
Quick Repeat chips — the coloured chips above the expense form show your most recent expense names. Tap one to instantly pre-fill description, category, and amount.
✂️
Split expenses — did one payment cover multiple categories, like a grocery run with Food and Household items? Enable ⊕ Split and divide the total. It logs as separate entries automatically.
🔁
Recurring transactions — set up rent, EMIs, and subscriptions once on the Add Entry tab. When they are due, hit Log Now — the transaction is recorded and the next due date advances automatically.
🛡️
Safe deletes everywhere — every delete action (transactions, goals, categories, recurring entries, IOwe entries, bill reminders) shows a confirmation dialog with context before removing anything.
📈
Track investments separately — use the Investment panel for SIPs, stocks, and FDs. These count toward your net savings but not your expense total. Dashboard shows your invest rate as a percentage.
📥
Bulk CSV import — have old bank data in Excel? Export as CSV with columns: Date, Type, Description, Category, Amount, Note — then import in App Settings to add everything at once.
🔥
Build a streak — log at least one entry every day to keep your streak alive. The Daily View habit score rewards consistency. Even a small purchase counts.

Common Questions
💾
Where is my data stored? Entirely in your browser's localStorage — it never leaves your device. Clearing browser data or switching browsers will erase it. Always keep a JSON backup from App Settings.
📱
Can I use this on mobile? Yes — open the HTML file in any mobile browser. On iOS or Android, tap "Add to Home Screen" for a full app-like experience with no browser chrome.
🔄
How do I sync between devices? Export JSON on one device, share the file via WhatsApp, email, or Drive, then import it on the other device from App Settings.
✏️
Can I edit a past transaction? Yes — tap ✎ on any entry in the Add Entry recent lists, Daily View detail panel, or All Transactions table. The edit modal lets you change every field including date and category.
🎨
Can I change the look? Yes — go to App Settings, scroll to Appearance. Toggle dark or light mode and choose from 6 colour themes. Changes apply instantly across all tabs.
🗑️
Will deleting always ask me first? Yes — every destructive action shows a confirmation dialog describing exactly what will be removed, with a Cancel button so you always have a chance to reconsider.
